Welcome to Taniti

A Small Island with an Infinite Soul

Taniti is a small, tropical island in the Pacific. In less than 500 square miles you'll find sandy and rocky beaches, a safe harbor, lush tropical rainforests, and a mountainous interior crowned by a small, active volcano. Whether you're here for adventure, romance, or pure relaxation — Taniti delivers.

Travel Planning

Getting to Taniti

Everything you need to know about arriving on the island and getting around once you're here.

Home › Getting Here
Arriving on Taniti

How to Reach the Island

✈️
By Air (Primary)
Almost all visitors arrive by air. Taniti's airport accommodates small jets and propeller planes. The airport is currently being expanded to welcome larger jets within the next few years.
🚢
By Cruise Ship
A small cruise ship docks at Yellow Leaf Bay's safe harbor one night per week. Check your cruise itinerary for Taniti's port day schedule.
Getting Around the Island

Ground Transportation

🚌
Public Bus
Public buses serve Taniti City and run daily from 5 a.m. to 11 p.m. Private buses serve the rest of the island. Affordable and convenient for city exploration.
🚕
Taxis
Taxis are available throughout Taniti City. Convenient for quick trips, late nights (buses stop at 11 p.m.), and travelers with luggage.
🚗
Rental Cars
Rental cars are available from a local agency near the airport. The best option for exploring the full island, including rainforest trailheads and the volcano.
🚲
Bike Rentals
Bikes and helmets are available to rent from several vendors. Helmets are required by law. Taniti City is flat and very walkable — bikes are a wonderful way to explore.
🚶
Walking
Taniti City is fairly flat and very walkable. The Merriton Landing area is also easy to explore entirely on foot — most activities are within walking distance of each other.
🤝
Car Share
Car share services are available for visitors who prefer not to rent a full vehicle. A flexible option for day trips and island exploration.
Practical Information

Before You Arrive

💵 Currency

Taniti uses the U.S. dollar. Many businesses also accept euros and yen. Several banks facilitate currency exchange and most businesses accept major credit cards.

🔌 Power Outlets

Power outlets are 120 volts — the same as in the United States. U.S. travelers will not need an adapter.

🗣️ Language

Many younger Tanitians speak fluent English. Very little English is spoken in rural areas, especially by older residents. The hospital has many multilingual employees.

🏥 Healthcare

There is one hospital and several clinics on the island. The hospital has multilingual staff. Violent crime is very rare, though petty theft is increasing with tourism growth.

The Island

About Taniti

A small island with a big heart — learn about the land, the people, and the culture that make Taniti unforgettable.

Home › About Taniti
The Land

Geography & Nature

Taniti is a small, tropical island in the Pacific. Despite an area of less than 500 square miles, the terrain is strikingly varied: sandy and rocky beaches, a small but safe harbor, lush tropical rainforests, and a mountainous interior crowned by a small, active volcano. The island's topographical diversity is one of its greatest attractions.

🏝
The People

Culture & Community

Taniti has an indigenous population of about 20,000. Until a recent increase in tourism, most of the Tanitian economy was dominated by fishing and agriculture. The island's culture is deeply rooted in its maritime heritage, and visitors are warmly welcomed as guests of the island community. Most of the tourist areas are centered on Taniti City and the rapidly developing Merriton Landing district.

🎭
Frequently Asked Questions

Good to Know Before You Go

What currency is used on Taniti? ▾
Taniti uses the U.S. dollar as its currency. However, many businesses will also accept euros and yen. Several banks facilitate currency exchange, and many businesses accept major credit cards.
What are the alcohol rules? ▾
Alcohol is not allowed to be served or sold between the hours of midnight and 9:00 a.m. The drinking age on Taniti is 18 and is not strictly enforced. Many pubs and the microbrewery are located in Merriton Landing.
Do I need a power adapter? ▾
No. Power outlets are 120 volts — the same as in the United States. Travelers from the U.S. will not need an adapter or converter.
Is English spoken on the island? ▾
Many younger Tanitians speak fluent English. Very little English is spoken in rural areas, especially by older residents. The hospital has many multilingual employees, and most tourist-facing businesses in Taniti City and Merriton Landing communicate well in English.
Is Taniti safe for tourists? ▾
Violent crime is very rare on Taniti. However, as tourism increases, there are more reports of pickpocketing and other petty crimes. Exercise normal urban caution with your belongings, especially in crowded tourist areas.
What healthcare is available? ▾
There is one hospital and several clinics on the island. The hospital has many multilingual employees. For non-emergency travel health concerns, clinics are conveniently located in Taniti City.
Are there national holidays that affect tourism? ▾
Taniti enjoys a large number of national holidays. Many tourist attractions and restaurants will be closed on holidays, so visitors should plan their itineraries accordingly. Check with your hotel or a local tourist information office for current holiday dates.
